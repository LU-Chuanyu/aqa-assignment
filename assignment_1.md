## Q3. Five JDs per role — requirement summary

The following tables consolidate JDs posted on company career sites and LinkedIn over the past 12 months. Employers sampled (5 per role):

- **Quant Developer (QD):** Citadel Securities, Jane Street, Goldman Sachs Strats, JP Morgan, Optiver
- **Quant Researcher (QR):** Two Sigma, D. E. Shaw, AQR, Man AHL, Morgan Stanley QSI
- **Quant Trader (QT):** Jump Trading, IMC, Tower Research, Citadel (Fundamental Quant), SIG

### 3.a Programming requirements

| Role | Must-have languages | Nice-to-have | Engineering expectations |
|---|---|---|---|
| Quant Developer | **C++ (modern: C++17/20, templates, concurrency, low-latency tuning)** or **Java**; Python required almost everywhere | Rust, Linux kernel tuning, kernel-bypass (DPDK, Solarflare), FPGA (HFT shops), kdb+/q | Multithreading, lock-free data structures, memory model; TCP/UDP/multicast networking; CI/CD, Git; TDD; LeetCode-Hard level algorithms |
| Quant Researcher | **Python (NumPy/Pandas/SciPy/scikit-learn/PyTorch or TF)**; ability to read/modify C++ is a plus | R, Julia, MATLAB, kdb+/q; Spark/Dask; SQL | ML pipeline, feature engineering, backtesting frameworks; ability to productionise research code for QDs; Jupyter + Git workflow |
| Quant Trader | Python (strategy prototyping, risk scripts); shell scripting | Read-only C++ ability; kdb+/q; Excel/VBA (some sell-side desks) | Live monitoring and parameter tuning of strategies; familiarity with OMS/EMS, FIX protocol, exchange APIs |

### 3.b Mathematics requirements

| Role | Core | Plus |
|---|---|---|
| Quant Developer | Discrete math, algorithm complexity, numerical methods (rounding error, stability) | Linear algebra, probability; finance math helps communication but is rarely required |
| Quant Researcher | **Probability & mathematical statistics** (measure-theoretic is a plus), **linear algebra**, **optimisation** (convex / stochastic), **time series** (ARIMA/GARCH/state-space), **machine learning** (supervised, unsupervised, reinforcement) | Stochastic processes (Brownian motion, Itô), information theory, Bayesian statistics, causal inference |
| Quant Trader | Probability & statistics (conditional probability, Bayes, expectation, variance), **mental-math and game puzzles** (poker, dice, market-making puzzles) | Stochastic processes, game theory; intuition for option Greeks and implied volatility |

### 3.c Financial-knowledge requirements

| Role | Required products / concepts | Plus |
|---|---|---|
| Quant Developer | Familiarity with at least one asset class (equities / futures / options / FX / rates); order book, matching engine, basic market-microstructure concepts | Option pricing (BS/Heston), fixed income (yield curve, duration, convexity), clearing & settlement |
| Quant Researcher | **Equity factor models (Fama–French, Barra)**, **option pricing and Greeks**, **statistical arbitrage and pairs trading**, **risk models (VaR, ES, tracking error)**, **backtesting pitfalls** (look-ahead, survivorship, overfitting) | Rates derivatives, credit derivatives, ABS, crypto assets, alternative data |
| Quant Trader | Greeks (Δ, Γ, Vega, Θ), volatility surface, arbitrage strategies (basis, calendar, cross-product), market-making fundamentals (inventory risk, adverse selection) | Corporate actions (dividends, splits), macro calendar impact, maker-taker fee structures |

## Q5. Open questions / doubts

These are the open questions that came up while completing Q1–Q4 and that deserve further discussion with the instructor or industry practitioners:

1. **Blurred business boundaries**
   - Multi-strategy firms such as Millennium and Citadel run equity L/S, statistical arbitrage, FI relative value and commodity macro under one roof. Should they be classified as Relative Value or as a separate "Multi-strategy" category? Is there a single industry-accepted definition?
   - What is the actual operational relationship between Citadel (hedge fund) and Citadel Securities (market maker) in terms of research, tech stack and risk management? Are they truly "fully separate"?

2. **Data availability**
   - Most US hedge-fund AUM data comes from Form ADV on SEC EDGAR; non-US managers (FCA in the UK, SFC in Hong Kong) are much less transparent. Is there a systematic way to discover them?
   - LinkedIn profile counts almost always underestimate true headcount. How can one correct for this bias?

3. **Job-title ambiguity**
   - The same "Quant Researcher" title means very different things at RenTech, Two Sigma and AQR (high-frequency signals vs. risk premia vs. factor investing). JDs rarely make this clear — is there a finer taxonomy (HFT QR / Mid-Freq QR / Factor QR)?
   - What are the substantive differences between sell-side "Strats" (Goldman/JPM) and buy-side Quants, and how do their career paths differ?

4. **Tech-stack choices**
   - HFT shops use very different languages — OCaml (Jane Street), C++ (Jump, HRT), Rust (newer firms). Which should a student prioritise?
   - kdb+/q is still the de facto standard on the sell side and at macro funds, but is barely taught at university. What are the recommended learning resources?

5. **Career path**
   - Five to ten years out, how do QT and QR career trajectories diverge? Is it true that QTs are more likely to become PMs in their 30s while QRs are more likely to be cut on a multi-PM platform?
   - For students without a STEM PhD, what realistic paths remain into firms like RenTech or D. E. Shaw?
   - What are the licensing and visa thresholds for crossing borders (US ↔ HK/China, HK/China ↔ US)?

6. **Industry trends**
   - How much will Generative AI / LLMs disrupt traditional statistical arbitrage and NLP-based alpha factors? How will required skill sets change?
   - Have crypto assets become mainstream in quant funds? Are the campus-recruiting pipelines the same as for traditional asset classes?
